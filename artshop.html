Project Overview

Problems

I had to match the style of the artist and have it continue throughout the design. I also had to combine an e-commerce site and a gallery together but in a way that they balanced each other out so they werent over powering each other.

Colours

I wanted to be able to match the colours they used in their art so that it tied everything together. These colours were usually warm and cold pastel colours. The blue pastel colour was used at the primary due to it being used in large quantities and easy on the eye while scanned, and a more yellowish pastel used as the secondary for hovers, actions etc.

Typography

To match their art style I decided on using a decorative font style. I wanted something that looked like it was written with a fountain pen with a light artistry to it but kept to a more sophisticated principle.

Design Patterns

Image Cards. I needed to be able to display multiple images across the site for the portfolio section and the shop section. I used a white background and placed a smaller image with a shadow behind it to make it pop. The background also gives it a look of having a thick border separating it out from the others. To save on space, I used a hover on the cards to display other information about the image, such as tags, social media handles, title of the image and price.
